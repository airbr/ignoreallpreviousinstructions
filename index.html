Sometimes it can feel like the web is a place where any idea or energy gets scraped. It feels like our ideas and work can get scooped up by indifferent and profit seeking training models and chat programs.

I recently learned I had been scraped when I made a website for my wedding. I got curious and asked at the time before the wedding the Meta Llama Chatbot about my name. Sure enough, it said I was getting married on a particular day in the future that was correct and named my spouse too.

Knowledge of my wedding was on the web but I didn't really like that somehow that Meta knew and shared this: it did not even link back to my website.

I wondered and lamented that maybe the website I had used, The Knot, to make the website was being scraped and tokenized into a database somewhere. Should I just expect this on the web?

I am here to say No. There is not much search benefit to any AI models scraping - they are serving our content without ever sending users to us, people actually making stuff on the web.

So I made this website. It is an attempt to frustrate robots and enliven humans. No trackers, no ads, just HTML and CSS. I have curated a robots.txt file to limit any LLM crawler that still respects that file.

This website has no metrics, no point, other than to wish you a wonderful day and to consider how extractive web crawlers affect creativity. When we know what we publish gets scraped... are we really writing for our desired audience?
